## 1. Legal Foundation (Non-Negotiable)

### What You Must Register

**Business Entity:**
- **CV (Comanditaire Vennootschap)** or **Koperasi (Cooperative)**
  - CV: Simpler, partnership structure
  - Koperasi: Better for profit-sharing model long-term
- **NIB** (Business ID) through OSS system
- **NPWP** (Tax ID)
- **TDUP** (Tourism business registration with Dinas Pariwisata Sulteng)

**Cost:** IDR 5-10 million setup (~$300-600 USD)  
**Time:** 2-4 weeks

### Tax Reality (Not as Bad as You Think)

**What you actually pay:**
- **0.5% of gross revenue** (PP 55/2022 small business scheme)
  - Example: IDR 100 million revenue = IDR 500k tax (~$31 USD)
- **No PPN (VAT)** until you exceed IDR 4.8 billion/year (~$300k USD)
- **Personal income tax** for guild members on distributed profits (5-15% progressive)

**Tax optimization strategy:**
- Distribute profits to guild members (reduces corporate tax burden)
- Members pay lower personal income tax rates
- Invest heavily in training/conservation (legitimate business expenses)
- **Result:** 40-60% less total tax than traditional corporate structure

### Guide Licensing (Required)

**Every guide needs KTPP (Kartu Tanda Pendaftaran Pramuwisata):**
- Contact Dinas Pariwisata Sulteng for training schedule
- 3-7 day training course
- Cost: IDR 500k-2 million per guide
- Includes: First aid, customer service, local knowledge, basic English

**Why it's non-negotiable:**
- Platforms (GetYourGuide, Viator, TripAdvisor) require it
- Insurance won't cover unlicensed guides
- National parks (Lore Lindu) check licenses
- Legal protection if something goes wrong

**Your brand promise:** *"The brand is amateur. The safety isn't. Certified guides."*

### Insurance & Banking

**Required:**
- Business liability insurance
- Guide accident insurance
- Business bank account (for platform payments, legitimacy)
- Payment processing (Stripe, Wise, Midtrans - all require registration)

## 2. The Guild System Vision

### Core Philosophy

**Not a traditional tour company.** A professional guild network where:
- Guides progress through mastery levels
- Specialization paths create deep expertise
- Revenue is distributed based on contribution
- Governance is shared among members
- Career ladder keeps talent in Sulawesi Tengah

### The Four-Tier Structure

#### **Tier 1: Apprentice Guide (Magang)**
- New guides, learning the GOWANA way
- Shadows experienced guides
- Gets licensed, builds skills
- **Compensation:** Base daily rate + tips share + training covered

#### **Tier 2: Journey Guide (Pendamping)**
- Licensed, experienced, leads standard tours
- Specializes in 1-2 destinations
- Mentors apprentices
- **Compensation:** Higher daily rate + tips share + tour bonuses

#### **Tier 3: Master Guide (Ahli)**
- 3+ years experience, deep expertise
- Designs custom itineraries
- Represents GOWANA publicly
- **Compensation:** Premium rate + quarterly profit sharing

#### **Tier 4: Guild Partner (Mitra)**
- Senior guides become co-owners
- Profit-sharing partners with ownership stake
- Decision-making voice in business direction
- **Compensation:** Ownership stake + profit distribution

### Advancement Criteria

**Not just time served—demonstrated mastery:**

**Apprentice → Journey:**
- Complete X tours as assistant
- Get KTPP license + first aid cert
- Pass knowledge assessment
- Positive feedback from 10+ travelers

**Journey → Master:**
- Lead 50+ tours
- Specialize in 2+ destinations
- Mentor 2+ apprentices
- Maintain 4.5+ star rating
- Complete advanced training (WFR, conservation practices)

**Master → Guild Partner:**
- 3+ years as Master
- Demonstrated commitment to place and brand
- Business acumen
- Invited by existing partners (not automatic)

## 4. Revenue Distribution Model

### How Money Flows

**Example: IDR 8,500,000 tour**

```
Revenue: IDR 8,500,000

Distribution:
├─ Direct costs (40%): IDR 3,400,000
│  (accommodation, transport, food, permits)
│
├─ Guide compensation pool (25%): IDR 2,125,000
│  ├─ Lead guide (Master): IDR 1,200,000
│  ├─ Assistant (Journey): IDR 600,000
│  └─ Tips (shared): IDR 325,000
│
├─ Conservation fund (10%): IDR 850,000
│
├─ Operations/marketing (15%): IDR 1,275,000
│
└─ Profit/reinvestment (10%): IDR 850,000
    └─ Distributed quarterly to Masters/Partners
```

### Profit Distribution Formula

**Quarterly distribution based on:**
- **Contribution:** Tours led, roles filled
- **Specialization:** Skill premium for specialists
- **Mastery level:** Higher tiers earn more
- **Mentorship:** Training others adds value
- **Governance:** Active participation in decisions

**Paid as "profit sharing" or "partnership distribution"**
- Members report as personal income (lower tax rate than corporate)
- Transparent calculation shared with all members

## 5. Governance Structure

### Three-Tier Decision Making

**Guild Council** (Strategic)
- All Master and Partner level members
- Meets quarterly
- Decides: Standards, revenue formula, investments, partnerships
- **Requires:** 2/3 majority

**Specialty Branches** (Operational)
- Each branch self-governs
- Decides: Training requirements, quality standards, certifications
- **Requires:** Simple majority within branch

**All-Guild Assembly** (Constitutional)
- All members including Apprentices
- Meets annually
- Decides: Charter changes, major direction, conflict appeals
- **Requires:** 75% supermajority

### Conflict Resolution

1. Peer mediation (within specialty)
2. Guild Council arbitration
3. All-Guild vote (final appeal)

**No single person has unilateral power.** Including you, the founder.

## 7. The Risks (And Mitigations)

### Risk 1: Government Scrutiny

**Risk:** Alternative governance attracts investigation, especially if successful

**Mitigation:**
- Stay legally compliant (boring but essential)
- Frame as "professional development program" not "parallel economy"
- Pay taxes, keep clean records
- Build relationships with Dinas Pariwisata (they can be allies)
- Don't advertise as "tax avoidance"

### Risk 2: Execution Complexity

**Risk:** System too complex, never actually launch tours

**Mitigation:**
- **Start simple, evolve gradually**
- Year 1: Just run tours with 2-3 guides
- Year 2: Add first specialization
- Year 3: Formalize guild structure
- Year 5: Full multi-branch system
- **Don't build the cathedral before the chapel**

### Risk 3: Guild Members Leave

**Risk:** Invest in training, they leave and compete

**Mitigation:**
- **This is actually fine** (raising the bar is success)
- Build culture and compensation that makes staying attractive
- Non-compete for sensitive business info only
- Don't trap people (against guild philosophy)

### Risk 4: Tax Authority Challenge

**Risk:** Authorities say "these are employees, not partners" → back taxes owed

**Mitigation:**
- Structure properly from day one (Koperasi or CV with clear partnership agreements)
- Document ownership stake and decision-making power
- Keep immaculate records
- Consult tax lawyer who understands cooperatives
- Members must have real governance participation (not just on paper)

### Risk 5: Loss of Control

**Risk:** Guild makes decisions you disagree with

**Mitigation:**
- **This is the point** (building something that outlives you)
- Your role evolves: founder → steward → elder
- If GOWANA becomes self-sustaining, that's ultimate success

### Risk 6: Insurance Won't Cover Guild Structure

**Risk:** Insurers don't understand model, won't provide coverage

**Mitigation:**
- Start with traditional employment contracts
- Add profit-sharing as bonus structure
- Transition to partnership model once proven
- Work with insurance broker who understands cooperatives

- Anyone can hire guides

- Enables premium pricing
- **Expertise justifies value**

### 5. **Governance Must Be Real**

- Guild members need actual decision-making power
- Not just on paper (tax authorities will check)
- Distributed governance is the point
- **Build to outlive yourself**

### 6. **Brand Integrity is Non-Negotiable**

- Can't promise transparency while operating illegally
- Can't promise safety without insurance/licenses
- Can't promise fair wages without proper structure
- **Walk the talk or don't talk**
